AP Statistics

Welcome to AP Statistics! This course introduces students to the major concepts and tools for collecting, analyzing, and drawing conclusions from data. Students explore statistics through discussion and activities, and they learn to use technology to help them analyze data and check conditions.

Course Overview

AP Statistics is equivalent to a one-semester, introductory, non-calculus-based college course in statistics. The course covers four major themes:

  • Exploring Data: Describing patterns and departures from patterns
  • Sampling and Experimentation: Planning and conducting a study
  • Anticipating Patterns: Exploring random phenomena using probability and simulation
  • Statistical Inference: Estimating population parameters and testing hypotheses

    • Key Topics

    Unit 1: Exploring Two-Variable Data

    Explore relationships between two variables through scatterplots, correlation, and linear regression.

    Unit 1: Exploring One-Variable Data

    Learn to analyze distributions of quantitative and categorical data, including measures of center, spread, and position.

    Unit 2: Exploring Two-Variable Data

    Explore relationships between two variables through scatterplots, correlation, and linear regression.

    Unit 3: Collecting Data

    Understand methods for collecting data through sampling techniques and experimental design.

    Unit 4: Probability, Random Variables, and Probability Distributions

    Study probability concepts, random variables, and common probability distributions.

    Unit 5: Sampling Distributions

    Learn about sampling distributions and the Central Limit Theorem.

    Unit 6: Inference for Categorical Data

    Perform statistical inference for proportions using confidence intervals and hypothesis tests.

    Unit 7: Inference for Quantitative Data

    Conduct statistical inference for means using t-procedures and confidence intervals.

    Unit 8: Inference for Categorical Data - Chi-Square

    Use chi-square tests for goodness of fit and independence.

    Unit 9: Inference for Quantitative Data - Slopes

    Perform inference about the slope of a regression line.

    Calculator Information

    Students are expected to use a graphing calculator throughout the course. The TI-83/84 series calculators are recommended and will be used for demonstrations in class. Key calculator skills include:

    • Creating statistical plots and graphs
    • Calculating descriptive statistics
    • Performing regression analysis
    • Computing probability distributions
    • Conducting hypothesis tests and confidence intervals

    Assessment

    The AP Statistics exam consists of two sections:

    • Section I: 40 multiple-choice questions (90 minutes)
    • Section II: 6 free-response questions (90 minutes)

    Throughout the course, students will complete regular assessments including quizzes, tests, and practice AP exams to prepare for the May exam.
